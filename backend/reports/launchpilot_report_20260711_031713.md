## Founder Report

Your brand clarity score is 36.2, well below the 60 threshold. This means your messaging and positioning are not yet clear enough for marketing to be effective. The recommended path is to invest the first 16 days in branding experiments to boost clarity, then run low-cost marketing validation. Start with the Value Proposition Canvas (1 day, free) to map your customer's jobs, pains, and gains against your solution. Follow with a Brand Positioning Workshop (2 days, free) to articulate your unique value proposition. Then conduct Customer Persona Deep-Dive (5 days, ~$100) by interviewing 10-15 nonprofit decision-makers. After that, refine your messaging and visual identity with a Messaging Framework Sprint, Landing Page Copy Rewrite, and Visual Identity Audit (total ~$50). Once brand clarity improves, run Campus Interviews (adapt to nonprofit events), a Beta Waitlist Campaign, and Reddit/Forum Engagement to validate demand. Finally, do Targeted Cold Outreach to 50 prospects. Your total budget of $500 is sufficient for these low-cost experiments. Key risks: low brand clarity may make marketing inconclusive, your small team limits parallel work, and reaching nonprofits may require adapting channels (e.g., LinkedIn, conferences). Mitigate by focusing on one experiment at a time and leveraging free online communities. After 30 days, you should have clear evidence on whether your audience and value proposition resonate.
